About me

I am a computer scientist and mathematician interested in hidden variables and causal structures: when they can be detected, if they can be learned, and how they affect networks of opinions. I am currently pursuing a Ph.D in Computing and Mathematical Sciences at Caltech, supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

I am also a competetive runner. My personal best for the marathon is 2:15:26 and I was one of the youngest competitors at the 2020 US Olympic Trials Marathon.

Research

I work with Shuki Bruck and Leonard Schulman on topics related to machine learning, including:
  • Expert Graphs - A structure we have developed to study consistency in overlapping expertise in the presence of uncertainty.
  • Mixture Models and Source Identification - Learning an unobservable counfounding variable given assumptions about the visible variables.
  • Covariate Shift - Preparing a machine learning classifier for demographic differences between training data and deployment.
