About me

I am a computer scientist and mathematician pursuing a Ph.D. in Computing and Mathematical Sciences at Caltech. I am supported by a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship and an Amazon AI4Science Fellowship. During fall 2022 and winter 2023 I am in Tübingen, Germany interning at the Amazon Research Causality Group.

I am also a competitive runner. My personal best for the marathon is 2:15:26 at the Chicago Marathon and I was one of the youngest athletes at the 2020 US Olympic Marathon Trials. In 2022, I was the USA 50km National Champion. For more information, see running info.

Research

Machine learning is obsessed with accuracy, but traditional science embraces unexpected results as the source of new insight. My work investigate how errors in carefully engineered ML tasks can teach us about hidden structures in data. I'm generally interested in mixture models, high level data fusion, and stability to distribution shift - usually through the lense of causality. I work with Professor Jehoshua Bruck and Professor Leonard Schulman and recently completed an internship at the Amazon Research Causality group.

  • Expert Graphs: We have developed a structure to study consistencies and inconsistencies in partial, but overlapping expertise. As it turns out, this problem is deeply related to issues in voting theory, such as the Cordorcet Paradox.
  • Mixture Models for Causality: Interventional distributions acted on by a universal unobserveed confounder can be though of as a mixture model. Cardinality assumptions allow identification of within-component probability distributions, allowing access to inteverntional distirbutions that were previously considered unidentifiable.
  • Domain Adaptation and Transportability: I am interested in using causal principles to guide models that are robust to distribution shift in datasets.
